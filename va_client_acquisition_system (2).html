Foundation Setup

"Set Up Your Digital Presence to Look Like a Professional VA"

Before you reach out to anyone, your online profiles must already tell a compelling story. Clients search you. Clients judge you before they reply. This module makes sure you look like a professional they can trust.

Goal

Before you reach out to anyone, your online profiles must already tell a compelling story. Clients search you. Clients judge you before they reply. This module makes sure you look like a professional they can trust.

Step 1: Set Your Positioning Headline

Your headline is the first thing a potential client sees. It must do 3 things:

  • Say WHO you help
  • Say WHAT you do for them
  • Use words they use (not jargon)

Headline Formula:

[Role] | Helping [Target Niche] [Result You Deliver]

Real Examples:

✅ Virtual Assistant | Helping Coaches Manage Content & Admin So They Can Focus on Clients ✅ VA & Social Media Manager | Supporting Online Business Owners with Daily Operations ✅ Executive Virtual Assistant | Helping Busy Entrepreneurs Reclaim 10+ Hours Per Week ✅ Social Media VA | Creating and Scheduling Content for Coaches and Course Creators ✅ Bookkeeping VA | Helping Small Business Owners Stay Organized and Audit-Ready

What NOT to write:

❌ Virtual Assistant (too vague — every VA uses this) ❌ Freelancer | Open to Opportunities (sounds desperate, no positioning) ❌ Hardworking VA Looking for Clients (makes it about you, not them)

Step 2: Optimize Your LinkedIn Profile

LinkedIn is your #1 B2B client magnet. Here is the exact setup:

A. PROFILE PHOTO

  • Use a clear, bright, solo photo — face forward, smile, clean background
  • Avoid dark selfies, group photos, or pet photos
  • Wear something professional (smart casual is fine)
  • Free tool: Remove.bg for background removal

B. BANNER IMAGE (LinkedIn Cover)

  • Size: 1584 x 396 px
  • Include: Your name + role, 1–2 services, short tagline, website/Calendly link
  • Free tool: Canva (search "LinkedIn Banner" template)
  • Color: Use your brand colors. Yellow + Black works perfectly here.
Ferdinand Cortel Virtual Assistant | Social Media Manager 📅 Schedule a Free Discovery Call → [link]

C. ABOUT SECTION (STRUCTURE)

Write this in 3 short paragraphs:

Paragraph 1 — Who you serve and what you do: "I help online coaches and small business owners take back their time by managing the admin and content tasks that eat up their day. If you're spending hours on scheduling, posting, and organizing instead of serving clients — that's where I come in." Paragraph 2 — What you specifically do: "Services I offer: → Social Media Management (content creation, scheduling, engagement) → Email & Calendar Management → Canva graphic design for social and marketing → Research, data entry, and admin support → CRM and lead tracking setup" Paragraph 3 — CTA: "If you're a coach or business owner who needs reliable VA support, let's connect. Visit my portfolio at [yourwebsite.com] or send me a message directly here on LinkedIn."

D. FEATURED SECTION

  • Pin your portfolio website link here
  • Pin a case study or sample project PDF
  • Pin a post where you introduced yourself + your services

E. EXPERIENCE SECTION

Even if you have no VA experience, write your freelance journey as a business:

Position: Virtual Assistant & Social Media Manager Company: [Your Name] — Freelance Dates: [Start date] – Present "Providing VA and SMM services to coaches and entrepreneurs. Specializing in content creation, scheduling, admin support, and social media management. Portfolio: [link]"

Step 3: Optimize Your Facebook Profile

Many Filipino business owners use Facebook as their primary platform. Your profile must look clean and professional.

Profile photo: same as LinkedIn — bright, clean, professional
Cover photo: Simple banner with your name + services
Bio: "Virtual Assistant | Helping coaches and businesses with content, admin, and social media | [website link]"
Work section: Add your freelance work
Featured photos: Remove party/unrelated photos
Set "Add Friend" button visible
Post a pinned intro post

Pinned Intro Post Template:

I'm a Virtual Assistant and Social Media Manager helping coaches and online business owners with: 📌 Content creation & scheduling 📌 Email and calendar management 📌 Admin support & CRM tracking 📌 Canva graphics If you're a busy business owner who wants to stop drowning in tasks and start focusing on your clients — I'd love to connect! 🔗 See my portfolio here: [link] 📩 DM me or comment "INFO" and I'll reach out!

Step 4: Optimize Your Instagram (Optional But Powerful)

  • Username: Make it your real name or brand name (not random numbers)
  • Bio format: Virtual Assistant 🖥️ | Helping coaches & entrepreneurs manage content + admin | 📁 Portfolio → [link in bio] | 📩 DM for inquiries
  • Feed: Post 3–6 sample work posts + testimonials before outreaching
  • Story Highlights: Create folders for "Services", "My Work", "About Me", "Testimonials"

Sample Project System

"Build Proof of Work — Even Without a Single Client"

The #1 excuse that stops VAs from applying: "I have no experience." This module eliminates that excuse forever. You will build 3 real-looking sample projects that prove your skills — no client required.

Goal

The #1 excuse that stops VAs from applying: "I have no experience." This module eliminates that excuse forever. You will build 3 real-looking sample projects that prove your skills — no client required.

The secret: Clients don't care if it was paid work. They care if you can DO the work. A mock project done well looks identical to a real one.

What Is a Sample Project?

A sample project is a piece of work you created for a fictional (or real, unpaid) business — specifically to show a potential client what they'd get if they hired you.

It must:

  • Look polished and professional
  • Solve a real business problem
  • Be relevant to your niche
  • Be presentable in your portfolio and in DMs

FOR: Social Media Management

Project Title: "30-Day Instagram Content Plan for a Fitness Coach"

What to create:

  • A content calendar in Google Sheets with 30 days of post ideas (Date, Content Type, Caption, Hashtags, Status)
  • 6 Canva graphics for the fictional brand (1080x1080px): 2 motivational quotes, 2 tip/value posts, 2 promotional posts
  • A 1-page "Content Strategy Overview" PDF with target audience, content pillars, posting frequency, tools recommended

Fictional brand: "FitWith Maria" — an online fitness coach for busy working moms

FOR: Email Management

Project Title: "Email Management & Inbox Organization System for an Online Coach"

What to create:

  • A Gmail label system (screenshot or Loom walkthrough): Labels for Clients, Leads, Invoices, Newsletters, Urgent, Follow-Up + auto-filters
  • 5 email reply templates: Discovery call confirmation, Inquiry response, Follow-up after no reply, Payment received acknowledgment, "I'll get back to you" placeholder
  • An email tracker in Google Sheets (Sender, Subject, Date, Priority, Status, Notes)

Fictional brand: "Coach Sarah Rivera" — a life coach managing 50+ emails/day

FOR: Admin Support / Data Entry

Project Title: "Lead Tracker + CRM System Setup for a Network Marketer"

What to create:

  • Google Sheets CRM tracker with columns: Name | Platform | Contact Info | Date Contacted | Message Sent | Response | Status | Follow-Up Date | Notes
  • Populate with 10 fictional leads (realistic names, platforms, statuses)
  • A 1-page "How to Use Your CRM" guide with color coding (Green = client, Yellow = warm lead, Red = no response)

Fictional brand: "Mark's Network Marketing Team" — MLM leader managing 50+ leads a week

FOR: Canva / Graphic Design

Project Title: "Brand Kit + Social Media Templates for a Business Coach"

What to create:

  • A brand kit Canva file with logo, color palette (3–4 colors + hex codes), typography pairing, brand voice description
  • 3 Instagram post templates (quote template, tips carousel, promotional post)
  • 3 Story templates ("New post", Poll/engagement, "Book a call")

Export as PNG + PDF and add to portfolio.

FOR: Content Repurposing VA

Project Title: "YouTube Video → Full Content Suite Repurposing Example"

Take a real YouTube video and repurpose it into:

  • A written blog post (600–800 words, formatted with H2/H3 headings)
  • 5 Instagram caption posts with relevant quotes from the video
  • 3 Twitter/X posts (short punchy takeaways)
  • 1 LinkedIn article intro (200 words)
  • 1 email newsletter draft (for the video's audience)

Label it: "Content Repurposing Sample — [Video Topic]"

How to Present Your Sample Projects

PROJECT CARD FORMAT: Title: [Project Name] Client Type: [e.g. "Fitness Coach — Sample Project"] Problem Solved: [1 sentence] What I Did: [3–5 bullet points] Result / Deliverable: [What the client would receive] [Screenshot or mockup image] [View Full Project button → PDF or Canva link]
ACTIVITY: Create 1 complete sample project in your niche. Build the deliverable, export it as a PDF or set of images, and add it to your portfolio website. This is your proof of work.

Most VAs waste time searching randomly for clients. This module shows you exactly WHERE your clients are hiding and HOW to identify the ones who are actively looking.

Goal

Most VAs waste time searching randomly for clients. This module shows you exactly WHERE your clients are hiding and HOW to identify the ones who are actively looking.

PLATFORM 1: FACEBOOK GROUPS

This is the highest-volume source for Filipino VAs targeting coaches and entrepreneurs.

Top Facebook Groups to Join:

Group Type Example Group Names
Business Owners (PH) Business Owners Philippines, Filipino Entrepreneurs Network, OFW Business Owners
Coaches + Consultants Online Coaches Community, Life Coaches Philippines, Business Coaches Mastermind

How to Find Pain Points in Facebook Groups:

Use Facebook Group search and type keywords like:

"need help with" "looking for someone to" "overwhelmed with" "does anyone know how to" "need a VA" "looking for a virtual assistant" "anyone offer social media" "I'm swamped"

When you find a post like: "I'm a coach and I'm struggling to post consistently on Instagram. Anyone know a good social media manager?"

That's a buyer, not a browser. Message them immediately.

PLATFORM 2: LINKEDIN

Best for: corporate clients, consultants, course creators, coaches

What to do on LinkedIn:

  • Use the search bar and type: "coach", "consultant", "online business owner", "course creator"
  • Filter by: 1st and 2nd connections
  • Look at their profiles for signals: inconsistent posting, no portfolio website, mentions of being "busy" or "overwhelmed"
  • Check if they commented under posts about productivity or outsourcing

LinkedIn Search Strings:

"looking to hire VA" "hiring virtual assistant" "need social media help" "overwhelmed with admin" "trying to scale my coaching business"

PLATFORM 3: INSTAGRAM

Best for: coaches, creators, influencers, fitness/wellness brands

How to find clients:

  • Search hashtags: #coach, #businesscoach, #lifecoach, #onlinecoach, #entrepreneurlife
  • Look for accounts with 1,000–20,000 followers (sweet spot)
  • Scroll their feed — look for inconsistent posting, low-quality graphics, or posts that just stopped
  • Check their bio — "DM for coaching" but no link? They need a VA.

Red flags that signal they NEED a VA:

  • Their last post was 3 weeks ago
  • Their captions are short and rushed-looking
  • Their stories are unpolished / just iPhone photos
  • They're replying to every comment manually (means no team)
  • They have a Calendly link but no website

PLATFORM 4: TWITTER / X

Search for:

"looking for a VA" "need a virtual assistant" "hiring SMM" "does anyone know a good VA" "anyone recommend an SMM"

How to Identify "Hot" Leads vs. Cold Leads

Signal Lead Temperature
Posted asking for a VA or SMM 🔥 HOT — Message now
Posting consistently but mentioned stress/burnout 🔥 HOT — Warm outreach
Has a business, active but inconsistent content 🟡 WARM — Engage first, pitch after
Has a business but hasn't posted in 60+ days 🟡 WARM — Research first
Business owner, no signs of needing help ❄️ COLD — Skip or educate first

Outreach System

"Start Conversations That Turn Into Clients"

Getting clients is a numbers and quality game. This module teaches you 2 outreach methods — Direct Pitch and Warm Lead Approach — with real, tested DM scripts you can copy and personalize immediately.

Goal

Getting clients is a numbers and quality game. This module teaches you 2 outreach methods — Direct Pitch and Warm Lead Approach — with real, tested DM scripts you can copy and personalize immediately.

METHOD A: DIRECT PITCH APPROACH

Use when: The person posted that they're looking for a VA / SMM, or their bio + content clearly shows they need support.

When to send: Within 24 hours of seeing the post.

The formula:

  1. Open with their name (personalization)
  2. Reference how you found them
  3. State what you do + who you help (brief)
  4. Give them one reason to trust you (portfolio, past experience, sample work)
  5. Light CTA — not "hire me" but "let's explore if I can help"

Template 1 — Responding to a post asking for a VA:

Hi [Name]! I saw your post in [Group Name] looking for a VA — great timing! I'm a Virtual Assistant specializing in [social media management / admin support / email management] and I work specifically with [coaches / business owners / online sellers]. I've put together a portfolio here so you can get a feel for how I work: 👉 [your website link] If you're open to it, I'd love to have a quick 15-minute chat to see if I'd be a good fit for what you need. No pressure — just a conversation! Looking forward to hearing from you 😊

Template 2 — Cold but targeted direct pitch:

Hi [Name]! I came across your page and love what you're building with [their business/brand]. I'm a VA and Social Media Manager who helps coaches like you manage content and admin so you can focus on your clients instead of your inbox. I actually put together some sample work that shows exactly what I do: 👉 [portfolio link] Would you be open to a quick call to explore if I can support your business? Happy to share more details!

Template 3 — Facebook Group DM after seeing a pain point post:

Hey [Name]! I saw your post in [Group Name] about [specific problem they mentioned — e.g. "struggling to keep up with posting while running your coaching program"]. That's exactly the kind of thing I help coaches solve! I handle the content planning, graphics, and scheduling so you can stay visible without it taking over your schedule. Here's my portfolio so you can see my work: 👉 [link] Want to hop on a quick call this week? 😊

METHOD B: WARM LEAD APPROACH (Higher Conversion)

Use when: You've identified a potential client but they haven't directly asked for help. You build rapport before pitching.

The 3-Day Sequence:

  • Day 1 — Engage: Like their 2–3 most recent posts. Leave a genuine comment (not "Great post!" — be specific)
  • Day 2 — Engage Again: Like or react to their story. Reply to their story with a short, genuine message.
  • Day 3 — Send the DM: Reference your engagement. Show you've been paying attention. Make it feel natural.

Warm Lead DM Template 1:

Hey [Name]! I've been following your content for a couple of days and I really love the value you share — especially [reference a specific post they made]. I'm a Virtual Assistant and Social Media Manager and I work specifically with coaches. I couldn't help but notice that managing content alongside everything else you're doing sounds like a lot. I actually help coaches with exactly that — taking the content and admin tasks off their plate so they can focus on coaching. Here's my portfolio so you can see what I do: 👉 [link] Would love to chat if you're ever open to it! No pressure at all 😊

Warm Lead DM Template 2:

Hey [Name]! Saw your post about [specific thing they said — e.g. "feeling stretched thin between coaching calls and content"]. I felt that! I help coaches in exactly that spot — I come in as the VA who handles all the backend stuff so you stop feeling like you're doing the work of 3 people. I put together a portfolio here if you want to peek at how I work: 👉 [link] Happy to jump on a call whenever you're ready to explore it 😊

Warm Lead DM Template 3 — Instagram:

Hi [Name]! I've been following your page for a while now and I love your message around [their niche/topic]. Quick question — are you managing all your content yourself right now? I ask because I noticed your posting has been a little up and down (totally relatable as a business owner!), and I specialize in helping coaches stay consistent on social media. Here's my work if you're curious: 👉 [portfolio link] Even if it's not the right time, happy to connect!

OUTREACH RULES (ALWAYS FOLLOW)

Rule Why
Always personalize — use their name and reference their content Generic DMs get ignored
Never open with "I want to work for you" It sounds desperate
Always include your portfolio link Builds instant credibility
Send max 10–15 DMs per day per platform Avoid spam flags
Keep your first DM under 120 words Long first messages get ignored
Don't pitch in comments — DMs only Comments feel public and pushy
Follow up once after 3–5 days if no reply Most yeses come from follow-ups

FOLLOW-UP MESSAGE TEMPLATES

Follow-up after no response (Day 4–5):

Hey [Name]! Just checking in — I sent a message a few days ago about supporting your coaching business with VA and content services. Totally understand if the timing isn't right! I just wanted to make sure my message didn't get lost in notifications 😊 Here's my portfolio again if you haven't seen it: 👉 [link] Happy to chat whenever works for you!

Follow-up if they said "maybe later":

Hi [Name]! I know you mentioned timing wasn't right when we last spoke — just wanted to circle back in case things have shifted. I have a couple of new sample projects up on my portfolio if you want to take a look at what I've been working on: 👉 [link] No pressure at all — just keeping in touch! 😊
ACTIVITY: Write your own personalized Direct Pitch DM and Warm Lead DM. Save both in a Google Doc so you can quickly customize them before sending. Then send at least 5 DMs using the warm lead approach this week.

Discovery Call Closing System

"Turn a Conversation Into a Paying Client"

The discovery call is where you close. But most beginners either oversell, underprepare, or offer too fast without understanding what the client actually needs. This module gives you the exact script and structure to run a confident, professional discovery call.

Goal

The discovery call is where you close. But most beginners either oversell, underprepare, or offer too fast without understanding what the client actually needs. This module gives you the exact script and structure to run a confident, professional discovery call.

BEFORE THE CALL — PREPARE

5 things to do 30 minutes before the call:

  1. Review the client's social media and website (know their business)
  2. Have your portfolio open in another tab
  3. Prepare your rate sheet (your pricing, ready to share)
  4. Have a simple proposal template ready (Google Doc)
  5. Set up a note-taking doc for the call

THE DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (15–30 MINUTES)

OPENING (2 minutes)

"Hi [Name], thanks so much for taking the time to meet with me! Super excited to learn more about your business and see if I can be helpful to you. Before I share anything about myself, I'd love to understand your business a bit better — is that okay? I'll only take 15–20 minutes of your time."

Why this works: You're positioning yourself as a consultant, not a job applicant.

DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (8–10 minutes)

Ask these in a natural conversation flow:

UNDERSTANDING THEIR BUSINESS: "Tell me a little about what you do and who you serve." "How long have you been doing this? How has it been going?" "What does a typical week look like for you right now?" FINDING THE PAIN POINTS: "Where do you feel like most of your time is going right now?" "What tasks do you find yourself doing that you wish you didn't have to?" "Is there anything that keeps falling behind because you just don't have time for it?" "How are you currently handling [content / email / admin]?" UNDERSTANDING THEIR GOALS: "If you could remove 3 tasks from your plate tomorrow, what would they be?" "What would it mean for your business if those tasks were handled consistently?" "What have you tried before? Did it work?" BUDGET/READINESS SIGNAL: "Have you worked with a VA before? What was that experience like?" "Are you in a place right now where you're looking for regular support, or more of a project basis?"

PRESENTING YOUR SOLUTION (5 minutes)

"Okay, so from what you've shared — it sounds like the biggest challenge right now is [specific pain point they mentioned]. And that's actually exactly what I help with. Based on what you've told me, here's what I think would make the most sense for you: [Present 1–2 specific services relevant to their needs] For example, I could take over [specific task] so that you're not spending [X hours] a week on it. Here's actually a sample of that exact kind of work I've done: [Share screen OR send portfolio link in chat]"

HANDLING OBJECTIONS

"I'm not sure I have the budget right now":

"I completely understand — budget is always a real consideration. What I'd suggest is we start small: even 10–15 hours a month of VA support can make a big difference and it's a very manageable investment. What would be most helpful to start with?"

"I need to think about it":

"Of course! No rush at all. Can I ask — is there any specific concern I can clarify for you right now? Sometimes the hesitation is something simple I can address."

"I've had bad experiences with VAs before":

"I really appreciate you sharing that — that's actually very common and it usually comes down to unclear expectations or poor communication. The way I work is very different — I do a full onboarding process, I check in regularly, and I'm very clear on timelines and deliverables. Would it help if I walked you through how my process works?"

CLOSING (3–5 minutes)

"Based on our conversation, I think there's a really strong fit here. I'd love to send you a simple proposal today — it'll have the specific services we talked about, the pricing, and next steps. Would you be open to reviewing it and getting back to me by [specific day]? And just so I can set it up correctly — is there anything you want me to make sure I include in the proposal?"
